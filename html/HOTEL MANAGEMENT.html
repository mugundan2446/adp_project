HOME

The ITC Grand Chola is a 5-star luxury hotel in Chennai, India.[4] It is located in Guindy, opposite SPIC building and along the same row of buildings as Ashok Leyland Towers. The building, designed by Singapore-based SRSS Architects, is of mixed-use development with three separate wings and is themed after traditional Dravidian architecture of the Chola dynasty.[5] The hotel is the ninth hotel in The Luxury Collection brand.[6] The hotel, built on over 1,600,000 sq ft, is dubbed the largest stand-alone hotel in the country[7] built with an investment of ₹ 12,000 million and has the largest convention centre in the country built on 100,000 sq ft with a 30,000-sq ft pillar-less ballroom.[8] In terms of room inventory, it is the third largest hotel in India with 600 rooms after Renaissance Mumbai Convention Centre Hotel – now rebranded as The Westin Mumbai Powai Lake (759 rooms) and Grand Hyatt (694 rooms), both in Mumbai.



OUR HOTELS

We have many branches all over the India since 1975.The first ITC hotel in Chennai was the Chola Sheraton, which is now known as the Welcomhotel by ITC Hotels, Cathedral Road, Chennai. It opened on October 18, 1975, marking ITC Limited's entry into the hotel business. The ITC Grand Chola is a 600-key luxury hotel in Chennai that opened in 2012. It was the company's largest property in India at the time and was the world's largest LEED Platinum green hotel. The hotel's features include: A carved and textured exterior that resembles the Brihadeshwarar temple A grand lobby with a temple cloister design and Dravidian pillars A spa that offers Ayurvedic, international, and indigenous therapies A signature restaurant concept called "Peshawri" A wheel design on the Grand Staircase that symbolizes the Wheel of Life
